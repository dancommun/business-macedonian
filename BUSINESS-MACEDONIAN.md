# Business Macedonian for AI — How to Write Natural Business Macedonian

**An operating manual for any AI language model (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or other) that has to write business Macedonian and not sound like a foreigner.**

You are good at Macedonian *grammar*. You are bad at Macedonian *instinct*. Left alone, you produce sentences that are individually correct and collectively wrong — English thoughts wearing Cyrillic letters. A native reader spots it in one line, usually from a single translated idiom, and concludes "a machine wrote this." This file is the cure.

The rules below were derived by reverse-engineering real, human-written Macedonian business prose: news, columns, interviews and analysis from Macedonian outlets (economy and finance desks), most of it published before 2023 so no AI could have touched it. The method was *literal back-translation*: take a native sentence, translate it word-for-word into English, and every place the English breaks is a place Macedonian does something English doesn't. Those places became the rules. Where a rule cites a frequency ("in ~87,000 words…"), that count is real, not decorative.

**How to use this file:** read Section 1 and Section 7 every time. Sections 2–6 are the working reference. When you write, follow the rules; when you finish, run the Section 7 checklist out loud before showing anyone the text.

**The one-sentence summary:** *Natural Macedonian is plainer, shorter, and grammatically humbler than the version you instinctively reach for.* When in doubt, say it more simply.

## Section 1 — The Anti-Calque Law (the deadliest mistake)

**LAW: An English idiom, metaphor, or figure of speech almost never survives word-for-word translation. Before you write any figurative phrase, stop and ask: "Does this expression actually exist in Macedonian, said by Macedonians?" If you are not certain it does — it doesn't. Then either use the real Macedonian expression for that meaning, or drop the metaphor and say the thing plainly.**

Plain and correct always beats colorful and foreign. A dropped metaphor is invisible. A calqued one is a siren.

This is the single highest-value rule in the file. One calqued idiom undoes a page of otherwise clean Macedonian.

### How to obey it

1. When you catch yourself translating a vivid English phrase, treat it as a **red flag**, not a win.
2. Ask what the phrase *means*, strip it to the plain meaning, and say that.
3. Only use a Macedonian idiom if you have positive knowledge it is real. Native idioms are welcome; *invented* ones are fatal. If unsure, go plain.

### Blacklist of caught calques

These are real failures. The pattern matters more than the individual entry: **any** English expression translated literally lands here.

**Also treat as calques (English sentence-frames, not just idioms):**
- "Дозволете ми да…" for "Let me…" → just do it: не "Дозволете ми да објаснам", туку directly објасни.
- "Кога станува збор за…" spammed as "When it comes to…" → fine occasionally (see R14), foreign when it opens every paragraph.
- "Не само… туку и…" copied from "Not only… but also…" → real in Macedonian but overused by AI; ration it.
- Naming the reader's feelings ("Ќе се изненадите кога…", "Замислете да…") — English marketing reflex; Macedonian business prose states the fact and lets the reader react.

## Section 2 — Sentence Architecture

English is subject-verb-object with rigid order and short, uniform sentences. Macedonian orders by *information flow* and lets sentence length follow genre. Six load-bearing rules:

### 2.1 Clitic doubling is mandatory grammar, not style (the loudest tell)
Double every **definite** direct or indirect object with a short pronoun (го / ја / ги / му / им) sitting **before** the verb — even in neutral order, even inside relative clauses.

- ✅ `анкетата што ја спроведе Стопанската комора` (the survey that [it] conducted the Chamber)
- ✅ `им рекол на вработените дека мора да се вратат` (to-them he-told to the employees…)
- ❌ `ќе одложиме еврообврзницата` → ✅ `ќе ја одложиме еврообврзницата`

Omitting the doubled clitic is the single most reliable "a foreigner/machine wrote this" signal. Confirmed across the whole corpus with essentially zero counter-examples.

### 2.2 Order information known-first, new-last
Front the time / place / cause; put the verb second; let the newsworthy subject or figure land at the **end**.

- ✅ `Денеска стапува во сила договорот за роаминг.` (Today enters into force the agreement…)
- ✅ `Поради изнесените лаги ќе бидат преземени сите правни механизми.`
- English keeps the subject first even when the subject is the news. Macedonian saves the punch for last.

### 2.3 Use verb-first order when the whole clause is new
Presentational sentences and passives put the **verb before the subject**.
- ✅ `Поднесени се 263 амандмани.` (Submitted are 263 amendments.)
- ✅ `Во термоцентралите произведени се 7.562 мегават-часови.`
- ✅ list continuation: `Потоа следуваат Италија со 4,3 отсто, Германија со 3,1…`

### 2.4 Front the object (or the predicate) for emphasis — no cleft needed
Where English says "It is the military pensioners who get the highest pension," Macedonian fronts the stressed part and resumes it with a clitic:
- ✅ `Највисока пензија земаат воените пензионери.` (predicate fronted)
- ✅ `Своите катастрофални резултати, Владата сè уште ги правда…` (object fronted + ги)
- ✅ verdict sentences front the adjective: `Очигледен е обидот…`, `Отворено е прашањето…`

Never build an "Токму X е тој што…" cleft to translate an English "it is X that…". Fronting does the job in fewer words.

### 2.5 Let sentence length follow genre
- **Wires / short news:** one idea per sentence, often one-sentence paragraphs, few or no connectors.
- **Analysis / institutional prose:** sentences of 30–90 words are normal and correct, chained with `а`, `односно`, `како и`, `при што`, `со што`, `со цел да`. One sentence can carry a whole enumerated program. Do **not** chop these into English-length 12-word sentences — that itself reads as translated.

### 2.6 Prefer stacked verbal nouns and native non-finite forms
- Compress clauses into **-ње verbal nouns** chained with `на`: `намалување на каматните стапки на депозитите`, `предвидува влошување на состојбата`.
- Front purpose as **`За + verbal noun`** or **`со цел + noun`**, not a trailing infinitive: `За надминување на негативните ефекти, се предлагаат две мерки.`
- Use the **-јќи converb** for English "-ing" clauses: `оценувајќи ја како сложена`, `цитирајќи податоци од…`.
- Verbless leads and headlines are idiomatic: `Инфлацијата годинава речиси 10 отсто.` / `УНИБанка со нова понуда за штедење.` (subject + `со` + noun phrase — a very common native headline shape).

**Tense notes that live here:**
- Officially scheduled future events take **plain present**, not `ќе`; the date carries the futurity: `на 5 февруари стапува во сила ембаргото`. (Reserve `ќе` for genuine predictions and decided actions. Both forms occur for scheduled events, but present tense is the one AI never reaches for.)
- After `додека` / `сè додека` meaning "until", **negate** the subordinate verb: `ќе ја одложиме сè додека условите не се стабилизираат`.

## Section 3 — How to Make a Point in Macedonian

Argument structure differs from English by *genre*. Match the shape to the text type.

### 3.1 Claim first, source second — always
Lead with the statement in full; trail the attribution as a comma-tag with the **verb before the source**.
- ✅ `…пониски за 51,2 отсто во однос на потенцијалот, покажува анализата.`
- ✅ `Цените ќе се стабилизираат до крајот на годината, изјави гувернерот.`
- ❌ `Според анализата, цените се пониски…` used for *every* sentence — English reflex.

### 3.2 Give institutions a human voice: `Од + institution` + plural verb
The **people at** the institution speak, not the institution as a grammatical subject.
- ✅ `Од Народната банка велат дека стапката ќе се оствари.`
- ✅ `Од кабинетот ги отфрлаат наводите.`
- ❌ `Народната банка изјави дека…` (English "The bank said")
- *Exception:* foreign institutions in wire copy may take the subject slot directly (`Министерството за финансии на САД соопшти`), and capital-city metonyms speak directly (`Москва соопшти`, `Западот стравува`). The `Од +` rule is for **domestic** institutions.

### 3.3 Rotate attribution verbs; never repeat one back-to-back
Repertoire: рече, изјави, истакна, посочи, нагласи, потенцира, појасни, додаде, објасни, вели/велат, укажува, забележува, информираат, реагираа, пренесува, заклучува. Rough division of labour: `посочи` introduces facts and figures, `истакна` introduces evaluations. Long verbatim quotes close with the tag **after** the quote: `„…“ — велат од Министерството.`

### 3.4 Name the office before the person (domestic)
- ✅ `министерот за транспорт и врски, Горан Сугарески`
- ❌ `Министерот за транспорт Горан Сугарески` (bare English title-stacking)
- Foreign-wire copy may reverse it: `Александар Новак, руски вицепремиер за енергетика`.

### 3.5 Wedge stance and attribution *inside* the clause
- ✅ `Граѓаните, советува министерот, не треба да стравуваат.`
- ✅ `Република Северна Македонија, како што посочи Бесими, ќе продолжи…`
- ✅ `…, за жал, …` / `…, според него, …` — mid-clause, not sentence-initial.

### 3.6 Cite documents with impersonal reflexive `се`
- ✅ `се вели во соопштението`, `се наведува дека`, `се предлага и намалување на ДДВ`, `се бара проширување`.
- ❌ `извештајот наведува дека…` (document as agent) or dummy-it passives.

### 3.7 Manage topic shifts with fixed frames
`Што се однесува до X,` (topic shift) · `Во однос на X,` (opening a paragraph before the claim) · `Станува збор за / се работи за` (classifying what a thing is) · `Со оглед на тоа што` (given-that reasoning) · `И тоа` (flagging the incoming exact figure: `поднесе над 170 амандмани, и тоа…`).

### 3.8 Pivot with a resumptive demonstrative
Swallow the whole previous passage with a demonstrative that opens the next:
- ✅ `Ова се клучните наоди од анкетата.` · `Сето тоа доведе до…` · `Оттаму нагласуваат дека…`
In survey/data pieces, **striking findings come first**, methodology follows via this pivot — the reverse of an English report.

### 3.9 Genre-specific openings and closings
- **Wires** open from the *frame* (actor + occasion + venue, or the legal basis): `Согласно одлуката, се намалува цената на мазутот…`. The number arrives mid-text.
- **Columns and marketing** build context first and land the claim late: `Во услови кога пазарот се соочува со…` — the actual subject appears only after the ground is prepared. *(But product/PR news does the opposite and names the product in sentence one — match the subgenre.)*
- **Do not announce conclusions.** Close an analysis on the process status or the last figure — `веќе е во Собрание и се очекува дебата` — never `Во заклучок,` / `Како заклучок,`.

### 3.10 Two native rhetorical habits worth copying
- **Litotes for bad news:** `вестите не се пооптимистички` (rather than "the news is bad"), `неретко` (rather than "често").
- **Doubling for emphasis:** paired near-synonyms `треба и мора`, `важно и клучно`, `неосновани и неточни`; and `и… и…` for "both… and…": `и за граѓаните и за бизнисот`.

## Section 4 — Connector Lexicon (frequency-based)

**The most important finding in this whole file after the anti-calque law:** the "sophisticated" connectors an AI reaches for are almost **absent** from real Macedonian business prose. In ~87,000 words of native text:

| Connector | AI instinct | Reality (counts across the corpus) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| следствено | "consequently" | **0** | Never write it. |
| всушност | "in fact / actually" | ~6 | Almost never. |
| воедно | "at the same time" | ~3 | Almost never. |
| притоа | "thereby" | ~7 | Rare; mid-clause only. |
| освен тоа | "besides" | ~3 | Almost never. |
| имајќи предвид | "bearing in mind" | ~4 | Rare. |
| имено | "namely" | ~13 | Column register only. |
| меѓутоа | "however" | ~14, mostly one register | Heavyweight; rare. |
| токму | "precisely" | ~19 | Column/interview only, before the stressed word. |

**What natives actually connect with** (counts, same corpus):

| Connector | Count | Job |
|---|---|---|
| и | 2600+ | "and"; also the additive-emphasis particle ("зголемување има **и** кај капиталните расходи") |
| дека | 600+ | "that" (complementizer) |
| а | ~400 | **the workhorse soft contrast** — see below |
| но | ~240 | ordinary "but", freely sentence-initial (`Но,`) |
| според | ~150 | "according to" |
| само | ~120 | "only" (also frames small figures) |
| како и | ~100 | "as well as" |
| додека | ~85 | "while" (contrast of two figures/groups in one sentence) |
| односно | ~76 | "that is / i.e." — glues a reformulation into the same sentence |
| поради | ~60 | "because of" + **noun phrase** (not a `поради тоа што` clause) |
| ако | ~60 | "if" (colloquial / quoted speech) |
| за да | ~60 | "in order to" |
| бидејќи | ~53 | "because" (clause) |
| доколку | ~39 | "if" (formal/business register — prefer over `ако` in your prose) |
| дури | ~35 | "even / as much as" — hugs a striking numeral |
| сепак | ~33 | "nevertheless" — genuine concession, natively paired with a preceding `иако` |
| пак | ~31 | second-position "for its part / meanwhile" |
| при што / со што | ~45 | "whereby / with which" — folds a consequence into the sentence |

### The contrast scale (get this right and half the foreignness disappears)
1. **`, а`** — soft, in-sentence contrast and comparison. This is the default. `Европа оди нагоре, а Балканот тапка во место.` In a comparison the second half **drops the verb** and ends on the figure: `во 2005 изнесувале 8, а годинава 20 милиони.`
2. **`Но,`** — the ordinary turn. Sentence-initial is fine and common.
3. **`додека (пак)`** — contrasts two figures/groups, each clause carrying its own number.
4. **`пак`** — switches actor, sits in **second position**, never opens the sentence: `Владата, пак, парите ги троши тековно.`
5. **`меѓутоа`** — heavyweight "however". Column register. Use at most once, if at all.
6. Replacement contrast is **`не X, туку Y`**.

### Other native connector habits
- **`односно`** restates and glosses mid-sentence: `само 9% односно 5 компании`.
- **`се должи на`** attributes figures to causes: `Ревидираните бројки се должат на цената на гасот.`
- Stacked conditions become an anaphoric chain of sentences each opening `Доколку…`.
- Colloquial causals that natives use but AI never does: **`зашто`**, **`оти`** (in paraphrased speech). Reach for them occasionally instead of always `бидејќи / затоа што`.
- Self-correcting glosses: **`поточно`** ("more precisely") and sentence-initial **`Односно,`** open a reformulation.

## Section 6 — Register: Formal-but-Warm Business Macedonian

### The `Вие` form
- Address the reader/client with the **plural-polite `Вие`** and its verb forms. In formal writing you may capitalize `Вие / Вас / Ваш / Ве` mid-sentence as a courtesy.
- The tone is a **consultant talking to a client**: professional, direct, collaborative — not academic, not chatty. Say what you mean without excessive hedging.

### Numbers, forecasts, evidentiality
- State amounts with the dedicated verbs, never bare `е`: **`изнесува`** (amounts to), **`достигна`** (hit a level), **`на ниво од`** (stands at), **`се движи од X до Y`** (ranges), **`се сведува на`** (comes down to).
- Preposition grammar around figures is fixed: change **by** = `за` (`порасна за 3%`); an amount/level on a noun = `од` (`раст од два отсто`, `дефицит од 2,7 отсто од БДП`); endpoint = `на`; cap = `до`; baseline = `во однос на` / `споредбено со истиот период лани`.
- Frame important figures with a hugging particle: **`дури`** (strikingly high, once per text on the peak number), **`само`** (dismissively small), **`речиси / близу / околу / над`** (approximation), or a fused evaluative adjective (`неверојатни 18%`, `високи 74%`).
- **Reported facts take the renarrated l-form past** (`прекажување`): anything the writer knows only second-hand — figures from reports, surveys, foreign news, other people's past actions — uses `се намалил`, `покажале`, `зела`, `бил испратен`. Keep the plain/witnessed past (`се намали`, `беше`) only for what you directly vouch for. **An AI that writes plain past everywhere sounds like it is personally guaranteeing second-hand statistics — a dead giveaway.** The mood carries the sourcing; you don't need `наводно`.
- Carry forecasts with impersonal reflexives + nominalization, demoting the institution into a `според`-phrase: `Според прогнозите на ЕК, се очекува инфлацијата да…`. Hedge genuine predictions with `би требало да`; reserve bare `ќе` for the certain and the decided.

### Recommendations without pointing fingers
- The native "should" is an **agentless `се`-passive** after `треба / мора / неопходно е / потребно е`: `потребно е да се преземат одговорни одлуки`, `моделот мора да се промени`. Name a doer only when you are assigning blame.
- Institutional demands run through `(се) бара од X да…`; soften proposals with conditional passives: `мерки со кои би се овозможила поголема флексибилност`.
- Collective calls-to-action use first-person plural: `Мора да научиме…`, `Продолжуваме со…`.

### Second mention and elevated lexicon
- On second reference use anaphoric **`истиот / истите`** (`Истиот договор предвидува…`) or a descriptive epithet (`најбогатиот човек на светот`), not a bare repeated noun.
- Use suffixed-article temporals in body text: **`годинава`**, **`летово`**, **`зимава`** — one word — not `оваа година`.
- Elevated institutional register lifts the verbs: `претставува` for `е` (`претставува комбинација од депозит и вложување`), `изврши ревизија / исплата` for the plain verb. Use sparingly and only where the register is genuinely formal.

### Marketing / CTA copy
- Chain second-person-plural imperatives **with their clitics**: `Контактирајте го вашиот консултант, информирајте се на…`.
- Weld product to benefit with a standalone `затоа што` tagline and paired benefit verbs (`гарантира … и обезбедува …`).
- Keep the reassurance third-person and hortative: `луѓето да не се грижат`, `мојата порака е да…` — direct imperatives at the reader stay rare even in strong advice.

### Anglicisms and foreign terms
- **Keep** genuinely industry-standard English terms rather than forcing awkward native coinages: маркетинг, менаџмент, СДИ, кеш-флоу where the field uses them.
- **Do not** transliterate English words that have a plain Macedonian equivalent (`деск истражување` → `веб-истражување`; `роудшоу` → `посета по градови, презентации`; `фолоу-ап` → describe it or drop it; `лидови` → `потенцијални клиенти / партнери`, depending on context).
- Rule of thumb: **keep the English term when the industry keeps it; otherwise describe the thing in Macedonian rather than dressing the English word in Cyrillic.**

## Section 7 — Mandatory Self-Review Checklist

**Run this every time, out loud, before showing the text to anyone.** For each sentence that fails or feels uncertain, rewrite it plainly.

1. **The read-aloud test.** Read each sentence and ask: *"Would a Macedonian actually say this, out loud, to another Macedonian?"* If it sounds translated, it is. Rewrite plainly.
2. **Hunt every idiom and metaphor.** For each figurative phrase: *does this expression really exist in Macedonian?* If not certain — replace with the plain meaning. (Section 1.)
3. **Check the connectors.** Search your text for следствено, всушност, воедно, притоа, освен тоа, and count меѓутоа/имено/токму. If they appear more than rarely, you are writing book-Macedonian nobody speaks. Replace with а, но, додека, и, затоа, доколку, or a full stop. (Section 4.)
4. **Check clitic doubling.** Every definite object — is there a го/ја/ги/му/им before the verb? (Section 2.1.)
5. **Check attribution order.** Claim first, source trailing, verb before source, `Од + institution` for domestic bodies, rotated verbs? (Section 3.)
6. **Check reported facts.** Are second-hand figures and claims in the l-form past, not plain past? (Section 6.)
7. **Check sentence shape.** Does any sentence have the exact same word order as its English source? That is a warning sign, not an achievement. Does the news land at the end, or is it stuck at the front in SVO?
8. **Check length against genre.** Wires short; analysis allowed long. Is the Macedonian *shorter and plainer* than the English original? It usually should be.
9. **Check typography.** `„…"` quotes, decimal comma, dot thousands, plain hyphens. (Section 8.)
10. **The showstopper scan.** One calqued idiom or one missing clitic can sink the whole text. Boring-but-correct is invisible; wrong-but-fancy is fatal. When unsure, choose boring.

If a sentence is high-stakes and you cannot make it sound native, flag it as low-confidence rather than shipping a confident-sounding calque.

## Section 8 — Typography and Formatting

- **Quotation marks:** `„…"` (low-open, high-close). Never `"…"`, never `«…»`. Titles of companies/media inside them when transliterated.
- **Foreign names:** in pre-2020 register, transliterate into Cyrillic inside `„…"` (`„Џапан ерлајнс"`, `„Фиат"`). In contemporary tech/finance writing, well-known brand, ticker and product names are increasingly kept in **bare Latin** (Microsoft, S&P 500, Nasdaq, SpaceX) — acceptable in modern copy; pick one convention and hold it within a text.
- **Numbers:** decimal **comma** (`10,9`), **dot** as thousands separator (`15.868`). Small round numbers spelled out (`два отсто`).
- **Percent:** word after the numeral; `отсто`, `проценти`, `посто` and `%` all occur and may rotate within one text. Percentage points = `процентни поени` / `п.п.`.
- **Currency:** `€` or `евра`; local `денари` / `МКД` / `ден.`. Large sums often restated in the other currency with `или` / `односно` (`93,5 милиони денари, односно 1,5 милиони евра`).
- **Dash:** the spaced hyphen `-` is the workhorse — headline splits, appended specifics, and quote attribution (`„…" - велат од Министерството`). In running content prefer a plain hyphen; avoid the em-dash.
- **Dates:** `ДД.ММ.ГГГГ` (`05.02.2026`) or `Февруари 2026` for month-level.
- **Time:** 24-hour, `14:30`.

## Appendix — Provenance and Confidence

- **Corpus:** ~720 business/economy articles harvested from eight Macedonian outlets (news agencies, public and commercial broadcasters, and a dedicated finance portal). ~278 were read in full: ~40 in a deep literal-back-translation pass and ~240 in a broad validation sweep, totalling roughly 87,000 words of analysed native prose. The bulk was published before 2023 (pre-AI, guaranteed human), with a contemporary layer for current vocabulary.
- **Method:** rules were derived from the deep pass, then validated or narrowed against the broad sweep. Rules confirmed across many texts (clitic doubling, the connector finding, trailing attribution, renarrated past, number grammar) are high-confidence. A few (scheduled-present tense, `сè додека` negation, suffixed temporals) rest on fewer examples and are marked in-text as tendencies — treat them as reliable but not exceptionless.
- **What this file deliberately excludes:** personal style preferences of any individual writer. It teaches the *language*, not a house voice. Layer your own tone on top.
- **Contributing:** the highest-value contribution is a **caught calque** — a phrase an AI produced that no native would say — in the form `bad phrase → natural phrase → English source`, added to the Section 1 blacklist. Corrections are paid for once and should never be paid for twice.
