# Business Macedonian

When you write, translate, or review Macedonian for business, follow the rulebook in **`business-macedonian.md`** (bundled alongside this file). It was derived from ~143,000 words of real, human-written Macedonian business prose. Read it and apply it; the condensed core is below so you can start immediately.

## The non-negotiable core

1. **Never calque an English idiom.** If you are not certain an expression exists in Macedonian, it doesn't — say the plain meaning instead. One calqued idiom ruins the text (e.g. NOT „одговарајте од стомак" for "answer from the gut" — say „одговорете спонтано"). See the full blacklist in Section 1 of the guide.

2. **Skip the bookish connectors.** следствено, всушност, воедно, притоа, освен тоа are near-absent in real Macedonian prose; меѓутоа, имено, токму are rare and formal. Connect with **а, но, додека, и, затоа, доколку, според** or a full stop. Make `, а` your default soft contrast.

3. **Double every definite object** with a short pronoun before the verb (го/ја/ги/му/им): „ќе **ја** одложиме одлуката". Omitting it is the loudest foreigner tell.

4. **Claim first, source last**, verb before the source: „…, изјави министерот". For local institutions: „Од Министерството велат…", not „Министерството изјави". Rotate the verbs.

5. **Reported facts take the l-form past** („бројот се намалил", not „се намали") — plain past is only for what you witnessed.

6. **Numbers:** изнесува (not „е") for amounts; change BY = „за", level = „од"; decimal comma and dot for thousands (15.868,50); frame the striking figure with „дури", the small one with „само".

7. **Recommend agentlessly:** „треба да се направи", „потребно е да се преземат мерки". Name a doer only to assign blame.

8. **Register (`Вие` vs `ти`):** business writing is `Вие` throughout; marketing may drop to `ти` in slogans and buttons only. Never mix them in one paragraph.

9. **Typography:** „…" quotes (never straight "), decimal comma, plain hyphen (not em-dash).

10. **Keep it shorter and plainer than the English.** Before finishing, reread every sentence: *"Would a Macedonian actually say this out loud?"* Rewrite anything that sounds translated.

## Registers

The guide has dedicated sections for **marketing/sales** (CTAs, slogans, the „решенија" calque plague), **formal documents/proposals** (salutations, nominalized objectives, indirect commitment), and **email** (open with the reason, never „се надевам дека овој меил ве наоѓа добро"). Consult the matching section for the text type you're writing.

## Self-review

Always finish with the read-aloud test in Section 7. When genuinely unsure a phrase is natural, choose the plainest wording — boring-but-correct is invisible; wrong-but-fancy is a showstopper.
