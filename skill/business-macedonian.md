# Business Macedonian for AI — How to Write Natural Business Macedonian

**An operating manual for any AI language model (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, or other) that has to write business Macedonian and not sound like a foreigner.**

You are good at Macedonian *grammar*. You are bad at Macedonian *instinct*. Left alone, you produce sentences that are individually correct and collectively wrong — English thoughts wearing Cyrillic letters. A native reader spots it in one line, usually from a single translated idiom, and concludes "a machine wrote this." This file is the cure.

The rules below were derived by reverse-engineering real, human-written Macedonian business prose: news, columns, interviews and analysis from Macedonian outlets (economy and finance desks), most of it published before 2023 so no AI could have touched it. The method was *literal back-translation*: take a native sentence, translate it word-for-word into English, and every place the English breaks is a place Macedonian does something English doesn't. Those places became the rules. Where a rule cites a frequency ("in ~87,000 words…"), that count is real, not decorative.

**How to use this file:** read Section 1 and Section 7 every time. Sections 2–6 are the working reference. When you write, follow the rules; when you finish, run the Section 7 checklist out loud before showing anyone the text.

**The one-sentence summary:** *Natural Macedonian is plainer, shorter, and grammatically humbler than the version you instinctively reach for.* When in doubt, say it more simply.

## Section 1 — The Anti-Calque Law (the deadliest mistake)

**LAW: An English idiom, metaphor, or figure of speech almost never survives word-for-word translation. Before you write any figurative phrase, stop and ask: "Does this expression actually exist in Macedonian, said by Macedonians?" If you are not certain it does — it doesn't. Then either use the real Macedonian expression for that meaning, or drop the metaphor and say the thing plainly.**

Plain and correct always beats colorful and foreign. A dropped metaphor is invisible. A calqued one is a siren.

This is the single highest-value rule in the file. One calqued idiom undoes a page of otherwise clean Macedonian.

### How to obey it

1. When you catch yourself translating a vivid English phrase, treat it as a **red flag**, not a win.
2. Ask what the phrase *means*, strip it to the plain meaning, and say that.
3. Only use a Macedonian idiom if you have positive knowledge it is real. Native idioms are welcome; *invented* ones are fatal. If unsure, go plain.

**Also treat as calques (English sentence-frames, not just idioms):**
- "Дозволете ми да…" for "Let me…" → just do it: не "Дозволете ми да објаснам", туку directly објасни.
- "Кога станува збор за…" spammed as "When it comes to…" → fine occasionally (see R14), foreign when it opens every paragraph.
- "Не само… туку и…" copied from "Not only… but also…" → real in Macedonian but overused by AI; ration it.
- Naming the reader's feelings ("Ќе се изненадите кога…", "Замислете да…") — English marketing reflex; Macedonian business prose states the fact and lets the reader react.

## Section 2 — Sentence Architecture

English is subject-verb-object with rigid order and short, uniform sentences. Macedonian orders by *information flow* and lets sentence length follow genre. Six load-bearing rules:

### 2.1 Clitic doubling is mandatory grammar, not style (the loudest tell)
Double every **definite** direct or indirect object with a short pronoun (го / ја / ги / му / им) sitting **before** the verb — even in neutral order, even inside relative clauses.

- ✅ `анкетата што ја спроведе Стопанската комора` (the survey that [it] conducted the Chamber)
- ✅ `им рекол на вработените дека мора да се вратат` (to-them he-told to the employees…)
- ❌ `ќе одложиме еврообврзницата` → ✅ `ќе ја одложиме еврообврзницата`

Omitting the doubled clitic is the single most reliable "a foreigner/machine wrote this" signal. Confirmed across the whole corpus with essentially zero counter-examples.

### 2.2 Order information known-first, new-last
Front the time / place / cause; put the verb second; let the newsworthy subject or figure land at the **end**.

- ✅ `Денеска стапува во сила договорот за роаминг.` (Today enters into force the agreement…)
- ✅ `Поради изнесените лаги ќе бидат преземени сите правни механизми.`
- English keeps the subject first even when the subject is the news. Macedonian saves the punch for last.

### 2.3 Use verb-first order when the whole clause is new
Presentational sentences and passives put the **verb before the subject**.
- ✅ `Поднесени се 263 амандмани.` (Submitted are 263 amendments.)
- ✅ `Во термоцентралите произведени се 7.562 мегават-часови.`
- ✅ list continuation: `Потоа следуваат Италија со 4,3 отсто, Германија со 3,1…`

### 2.4 Front the object (or the predicate) for emphasis — no cleft needed
Where English says "It is the military pensioners who get the highest pension," Macedonian fronts the stressed part and resumes it with a clitic:
- ✅ `Највисока пензија земаат воените пензионери.` (predicate fronted)
- ✅ `Своите катастрофални резултати, Владата сè уште ги правда…` (object fronted + ги)
- ✅ verdict sentences front the adjective: `Очигледен е обидот…`, `Отворено е прашањето…`

Never build an "Токму X е тој што…" cleft to translate an English "it is X that…". Fronting does the job in fewer words.

### 2.5 Let sentence length follow genre
- **Wires / short news:** one idea per sentence, often one-sentence paragraphs, few or no connectors.
- **Analysis / institutional prose:** sentences of 30–90 words are normal and correct, chained with `а`, `односно`, `како и`, `при што`, `со што`, `со цел да`. One sentence can carry a whole enumerated program. Do **not** chop these into English-length 12-word sentences — that itself reads as translated.

### 2.6 Prefer stacked verbal nouns and native non-finite forms
- Compress clauses into **-ње verbal nouns** chained with `на`: `намалување на каматните стапки на депозитите`, `предвидува влошување на состојбата`.
- Front purpose as **`За + verbal noun`** or **`со цел + noun`**, not a trailing infinitive: `За надминување на негативните ефекти, се предлагаат две мерки.`
- Use the **-јќи converb** for English "-ing" clauses: `оценувајќи ја како сложена`, `цитирајќи податоци од…`.
- Verbless leads and headlines are idiomatic: `Инфлацијата годинава речиси 10 отсто.` / `УНИБанка со нова понуда за штедење.` (subject + `со` + noun phrase — a very common native headline shape).

**Tense notes that live here:**
- Officially scheduled future events take **plain present**, not `ќе`; the date carries the futurity: `на 5 февруари стапува во сила ембаргото`. (Reserve `ќе` for genuine predictions and decided actions. Both forms occur for scheduled events, but present tense is the one AI never reaches for.)
- After `додека` / `сè додека` meaning "until", **negate** the subordinate verb: `ќе ја одложиме сè додека условите не се стабилизираат`.

## Section 3 — How to Make a Point in Macedonian

Argument structure differs from English by *genre*. Match the shape to the text type.

### 3.1 Claim first, source second — always
Lead with the statement in full; trail the attribution as a comma-tag with the **verb before the source**.
- ✅ `…пониски за 51,2 отсто во однос на потенцијалот, покажува анализата.`
- ✅ `Цените ќе се стабилизираат до крајот на годината, изјави гувернерот.`
- ❌ `Според анализата, цените се пониски…` used for *every* sentence — English reflex.

### 3.2 Give institutions a human voice: `Од + institution` + plural verb
The **people at** the institution speak, not the institution as a grammatical subject.
- ✅ `Од Народната банка велат дека стапката ќе се оствари.`
- ✅ `Од кабинетот ги отфрлаат наводите.`
- ❌ `Народната банка изјави дека…` (English "The bank said")
- *Exception:* foreign institutions in wire copy may take the subject slot directly (`Министерството за финансии на САД соопшти`), and capital-city metonyms speak directly (`Москва соопшти`, `Западот стравува`). The `Од +` rule is for **domestic** institutions.

### 3.3 Rotate attribution verbs; never repeat one back-to-back
Repertoire: рече, изјави, истакна, посочи, нагласи, потенцира, појасни, додаде, објасни, вели/велат, укажува, забележува, информираат, реагираа, пренесува, заклучува. Rough division of labour: `посочи` introduces facts and figures, `истакна` introduces evaluations. Long verbatim quotes close with the tag **after** the quote: `„…“ — велат од Министерството.`

### 3.4 Name the office before the person (domestic)
- ✅ `министерот за транспорт и врски, Горан Сугарески`
- ❌ `Министерот за транспорт Горан Сугарески` (bare English title-stacking)
- Foreign-wire copy may reverse it: `Александар Новак, руски вицепремиер за енергетика`.

### 3.5 Wedge stance and attribution *inside* the clause
- ✅ `Граѓаните, советува министерот, не треба да стравуваат.`
- ✅ `Република Северна Македонија, како што посочи Бесими, ќе продолжи…`
- ✅ `…, за жал, …` / `…, според него, …` — mid-clause, not sentence-initial.

### 3.6 Cite documents with impersonal reflexive `се`
- ✅ `се вели во соопштението`, `се наведува дека`, `се предлага и намалување на ДДВ`, `се бара проширување`.
- ❌ `извештајот наведува дека…` (document as agent) or dummy-it passives.

### 3.7 Manage topic shifts with fixed frames
`Што се однесува до X,` (topic shift) · `Во однос на X,` (opening a paragraph before the claim) · `Станува збор за / се работи за` (classifying what a thing is) · `Со оглед на тоа што` (given-that reasoning) · `И тоа` (flagging the incoming exact figure: `поднесе над 170 амандмани, и тоа…`).

### 3.8 Pivot with a resumptive demonstrative
Swallow the whole previous passage with a demonstrative that opens the next:
- ✅ `Ова се клучните наоди од анкетата.` · `Сето тоа доведе до…` · `Оттаму нагласуваат дека…`
In survey/data pieces, **striking findings come first**, methodology follows via this pivot — the reverse of an English report.

### 3.9 Genre-specific openings and closings
- **Wires** open from the *frame* (actor + occasion + venue, or the legal basis): `Согласно одлуката, се намалува цената на мазутот…`. The number arrives mid-text.
- **Columns and marketing** build context first and land the claim late: `Во услови кога пазарот се соочува со…` — the actual subject appears only after the ground is prepared. *(But product/PR news does the opposite and names the product in sentence one — match the subgenre.)*
- **Do not announce conclusions.** Close an analysis on the process status or the last figure — `веќе е во Собрание и се очекува дебата` — never `Во заклучок,` / `Како заклучок,`.

### 3.10 Two native rhetorical habits worth copying
- **Litotes for bad news:** `вестите не се пооптимистички` (rather than "the news is bad"), `неретко` (rather than "често").
- **Doubling for emphasis:** paired near-synonyms `треба и мора`, `важно и клучно`, `неосновани и неточни`; and `и… и…` for "both… and…": `и за граѓаните и за бизнисот`.

## Section 4 — Connector Lexicon (frequency-based)

**The most important finding in this whole file after the anti-calque law:** the "sophisticated" connectors an AI reaches for are almost **absent** from real Macedonian business prose. In ~143,000 words of native text:

| Connector | AI instinct | Reality (count in ~143k words) | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| следствено | "consequently" | **4** | Never write it. |
| освен тоа | "besides" | 7 | Almost never. |
| имајќи предвид | "bearing in mind" | 6 | Rare. |
| всушност | "in fact / actually" | 13 | Almost never. |
| воедно | "at the same time" | 14 | Almost never. |
| имено | "namely" | 19 | Column register only. |
| притоа | "thereby" | 24 | Rare; mid-clause only. |
| меѓутоа | "however" | 14 | Heavyweight; rare. |
| токму | "precisely" | 35 | Column/interview only, before the stressed word. |

For scale: the plainest workhorse below, `а`, appears **615** times and `но` **352** — the "fancy" connectors barely register.

**What natives actually connect with** (counts across the same ~143k words):

| Connector | Count | Job |
|---|---|---|
| и | 4400+ | "and"; also the additive-emphasis particle ("зголемување има **и** кај капиталните расходи") |
| дека | 1000+ | "that" (complementizer) |
| а | ~615 | **the workhorse soft contrast** — see below |
| но | ~350 | ordinary "but", freely sentence-initial (`Но,`) |
| според | ~240 | "according to" |
| само | ~200 | "only" (also frames small figures) |
| како и | ~170 | "as well as" |
| за да | ~160 | "in order to" |
| додека | ~130 | "while" (contrast of two figures/groups in one sentence) |
| односно | ~130 | "that is / i.e." — glues a reformulation into the same sentence |
| поради | ~120 | "because of" + **noun phrase** (not a `поради тоа што` clause) |
| ако | ~90 | "if" (colloquial / quoted speech) |
| бидејќи | ~70 | "because" (clause) |
| доколку | ~66 | "if" (formal/business register — prefer over `ако` in your prose) |
| дури | ~60 | "even / as much as" — hugs a striking numeral |
| пак | ~57 | second-position "for its part / meanwhile" |
| сепак | ~55 | "nevertheless" — genuine concession, natively paired with a preceding `иако` |
| при што / со што | ~40+ | "whereby / with which" — folds a consequence into the sentence |

### The contrast scale (get this right and half the foreignness disappears)
1. **`, а`** — soft, in-sentence contrast and comparison. This is the default. `Европа оди нагоре, а Балканот тапка во место.` In a comparison the second half **drops the verb** and ends on the figure: `во 2005 изнесувале 8, а годинава 20 милиони.`
2. **`Но,`** — the ordinary turn. Sentence-initial is fine and common.
3. **`додека (пак)`** — contrasts two figures/groups, each clause carrying its own number.
4. **`пак`** — switches actor, sits in **second position**, never opens the sentence: `Владата, пак, парите ги троши тековно.`
5. **`меѓутоа`** — heavyweight "however". Column register. Use at most once, if at all.
6. Replacement contrast is **`не X, туку Y`**.

### Other native connector habits
- **`односно`** restates and glosses mid-sentence: `само 9% односно 5 компании`.
- **`се должи на`** attributes figures to causes: `Ревидираните бројки се должат на цената на гасот.`
- Stacked conditions become an anaphoric chain of sentences each opening `Доколку…`.
- Colloquial causals that natives use but AI never does: **`зашто`**, **`оти`** (in paraphrased speech). Reach for them occasionally instead of always `бидејќи / затоа што`.
- Self-correcting glosses: **`поточно`** ("more precisely") and sentence-initial **`Односно,`** open a reformulation.

## Section 5 — Business Phrase Bank

Natural collocations harvested from the corpus. Use them to replace stiff literal translations. `MK — EN meaning`.

### Growth, decline, performance
- бележи раст / пад од — registers growth / a fall of
- достигна рекордни нивоа — reached record levels
- историски највисоко ниво — record-high level
- оди нагоре — is on the way up
- го надмина поширокиот пазар — outperformed the broader market
- растечки тренд — a rising trend
- забележа загуба од — posted a loss of
- бизнисот е во позитива — the business is in the black
- останува предизвик — remains a challenge
- не ги дал очекуваните ефекти — did not deliver the expected effects
- ќе претрпи посилен удар — will take a stronger hit
- да се амортизира ударот врз — to cushion the blow to

### Money, debt, financing
- управување со јавниот долг — public debt management
- долгот достигнува загрижувачки вредности — the debt is reaching worrying levels
- се задолжила кај домашните кредитори — borrowed from domestic creditors
- преземање на долгот — assuming the debt
- изврши исплата на нова транша — disbursed a new tranche
- слободни средства — idle / available funds
- има блокирана сметка — has a frozen account
- на сметките легнаа … денари — … denars landed in the accounts (native payout phrasing)
- финансиска поддршка преку мерката — financial support via the measure
- по основ на — on the basis of (accounting head)
- новоодобрени кредити — newly approved loans
- проблеми со ликвидноста и солвентноста — liquidity and solvency problems

### Markets, trade, stock exchange
- обем на трговската размена — volume of trade exchange
- излез на странски пазар — entry into a foreign market
- синџир на снабдување — supply chain
- дисторзија / доминација на пазарот — market distortion / dominance
- котираат на берзата — are listed on the stock exchange

- просечна берзанска цена — average exchange price
- подложни на надворешни шокови — vulnerable to external shocks
- странски директни инвестиции (СДИ) — foreign direct investment (FDI)
- куповната / куповна моќ — purchasing power

### Prices, inflation, policy
- ценовен плафон — price ceiling / cap
- вештачко зголемување на цените — artificial price increases
- да ги закотви инфлациските очекувања — to anchor inflation expectations
- останува со непроменета цена — the price remains unchanged
- поскапува / поевтинува за — gets more expensive / cheaper by
- олабавување на мерките — loosening of the measures
- ќе работи со регулирани цени — will operate at regulated prices
- рафтовите остануваат празни — the shelves stay empty
- амортизира / апсорбира шок — cushions / absorbs a shock

### Companies, operations, deals
- меморандум за разбирање — memorandum of understanding
- план за реструктуирање — restructuring plan
- идејно решение — concept design
- ставен во функција / пуштен во употреба — put into operation
- еднострано раскинување на договорот — unilateral termination of the contract
- не го продолжи договорот за концесија — did not extend the concession contract
- пројавиле интерес за — expressed interest in
- земаме учество во — we take part in (formal)
- реномирани ревизорски куќи — renowned audit firms
- стекнаа доверба кај — earned the trust of
- се закани дека ќе ја тужи — threatened to sue it

### Labor, wages, jobs
- квалификувана работна сила — skilled workforce
- нов бран вработувања — a new wave of hirings
- се отворија … работни места — … jobs were created
- пополнување на испразнети работни места — filling vacated positions
- заостанати плати / заостанувања — wage arrears
- покачување / заостанување на платите — wage increase / lag
- месечна заработувачка — monthly earnings
- им следува на вработените — the employees are entitled to it
- финансиска поддршка за исплата на плата — support for salary payment

### Reform, institutions, procedure
- низа реформски активности — a series of reform activities
- унапредување на деловното опкружување — improving the business environment
- надминување на бариерите / последиците од кризата — overcoming the barriers / the crisis fallout
- законска обврска — legal obligation
- стапија на сила законските измени — the legal amendments took effect
- вон сила — no longer in force
- постапува по поплаките — acts on the complaints
- поднесе во писмена форма преку архивата — submit in writing via the registry office
- му дадоа рок на министерот — gave the minister a deadline
- врши политичка пресметка — is settling political scores

### Formulaic frames (openers, hedges, glue)
- во услови на растечка … криза — amid a growing … crisis
- во пресрет на (изборите / годишнината) — in the run-up to / on the eve of
- по повод — on the occasion of
- во најкус можен рок / на долг рок — in the shortest possible time / in the long run
- се очекува / се предвидува / се проценува — is expected / forecast / estimated (impersonal)
- би требало да / би можела да — should / could (hedged forecast)
- отворено е прашањето — the question remains open
- станува збор за / се работи за — it is a matter of
- да вродат со плод — to bear fruit (native idiom — safe to use)

## Section 6 — Register: Formal-but-Warm Business Macedonian

### The `Вие` form
- Address the reader/client with the **plural-polite `Вие`** and its verb forms. In formal writing you may capitalize `Вие / Вас / Ваш / Ве` mid-sentence as a courtesy.
- The tone is a **consultant talking to a client**: professional, direct, collaborative — not academic, not chatty. Say what you mean without excessive hedging.

### Numbers, forecasts, evidentiality
- State amounts with the dedicated verbs, never bare `е`: **`изнесува`** (amounts to), **`достигна`** (hit a level), **`на ниво од`** (stands at), **`се движи од X до Y`** (ranges), **`се сведува на`** (comes down to).
- Preposition grammar around figures is fixed: change **by** = `за` (`порасна за 3%`); an amount/level on a noun = `од` (`раст од два отсто`, `дефицит од 2,7 отсто од БДП`); endpoint = `на`; cap = `до`; baseline = `во однос на` / `споредбено со истиот период лани`.
- Frame important figures with a hugging particle: **`дури`** (strikingly high, once per text on the peak number), **`само`** (dismissively small), **`речиси / близу / околу / над`** (approximation), or a fused evaluative adjective (`неверојатни 18%`, `високи 74%`).
- **Reported facts take the renarrated l-form past** (`прекажување`): anything the writer knows only second-hand — figures from reports, surveys, foreign news, other people's past actions — uses `се намалил`, `покажале`, `зела`, `бил испратен`. Keep the plain/witnessed past (`се намали`, `беше`) only for what you directly vouch for. **An AI that writes plain past everywhere sounds like it is personally guaranteeing second-hand statistics — a dead giveaway.** The mood carries the sourcing; you don't need `наводно`.
- Carry forecasts with impersonal reflexives + nominalization, demoting the institution into a `според`-phrase: `Според прогнозите на ЕК, се очекува инфлацијата да…`. Hedge genuine predictions with `би требало да`; reserve bare `ќе` for the certain and the decided.

### Recommendations without pointing fingers
- The native "should" is an **agentless `се`-passive** after `треба / мора / неопходно е / потребно е`: `потребно е да се преземат одговорни одлуки`, `моделот мора да се промени`. Name a doer only when you are assigning blame.
- Institutional demands run through `(се) бара од X да…`; soften proposals with conditional passives: `мерки со кои би се овозможила поголема флексибилност`.
- Collective calls-to-action use first-person plural: `Мора да научиме…`, `Продолжуваме со…`.

### Second mention and elevated lexicon
- On second reference use anaphoric **`истиот / истите`** (`Истиот договор предвидува…`) or a descriptive epithet (`најбогатиот човек на светот`), not a bare repeated noun.
- Use suffixed-article temporals in body text: **`годинава`**, **`летово`**, **`зимава`** — one word — not `оваа година`.
- Elevated institutional register lifts the verbs: `претставува` for `е` (`претставува комбинација од депозит и вложување`), `изврши ревизија / исплата` for the plain verb. Use sparingly and only where the register is genuinely formal.

### Marketing / CTA copy
- Chain second-person-plural imperatives **with their clitics**: `Контактирајте го вашиот консултант, информирајте се на…`.
- Weld product to benefit with a standalone `затоа што` tagline and paired benefit verbs (`гарантира … и обезбедува …`).
- Keep the reassurance third-person and hortative: `луѓето да не се грижат`, `мојата порака е да…` — direct imperatives at the reader stay rare even in strong advice.

### Anglicisms and foreign terms
- **Keep** genuinely industry-standard English terms rather than forcing awkward native coinages: маркетинг, менаџмент, СДИ, кеш-флоу where the field uses them.
- **Do not** transliterate English words that have a plain Macedonian equivalent (`деск истражување` → `веб-истражување`; `роудшоу` → `посета по градови, презентации`; `фолоу-ап` → describe it or drop it; `лидови` → `потенцијални клиенти / партнери`, depending on context).
- Rule of thumb: **keep the English term when the industry keeps it; otherwise describe the thing in Macedonian rather than dressing the English word in Cyrillic.**

## Section 7 — Mandatory Self-Review Checklist

**Run this every time, out loud, before showing the text to anyone.** For each sentence that fails or feels uncertain, rewrite it plainly.

1. **The read-aloud test.** Read each sentence and ask: *"Would a Macedonian actually say this, out loud, to another Macedonian?"* If it sounds translated, it is. Rewrite plainly.
2. **Hunt every idiom and metaphor.** For each figurative phrase: *does this expression really exist in Macedonian?* If not certain — replace with the plain meaning. (Section 1.)
3. **Check the connectors.** Search your text for следствено, всушност, воедно, притоа, освен тоа, and count меѓутоа/имено/токму. If they appear more than rarely, you are writing book-Macedonian nobody speaks. Replace with а, но, додека, и, затоа, доколку, or a full stop. (Section 4.)
4. **Check clitic doubling.** Every definite object — is there a го/ја/ги/му/им before the verb? (Section 2.1.)
5. **Check attribution order.** Claim first, source trailing, verb before source, `Од + institution` for domestic bodies, rotated verbs? (Section 3.)
6. **Check reported facts.** Are second-hand figures and claims in the l-form past, not plain past? (Section 6.)
7. **Check sentence shape.** Does any sentence have the exact same word order as its English source? That is a warning sign, not an achievement. Does the news land at the end, or is it stuck at the front in SVO?
8. **Check length against genre.** Wires short; analysis allowed long. Is the Macedonian *shorter and plainer* than the English original? It usually should be.
9. **Check typography.** `„…"` quotes, decimal comma, dot thousands, plain hyphens. (Section 8.)
10. **The showstopper scan.** One calqued idiom or one missing clitic can sink the whole text. Boring-but-correct is invisible; wrong-but-fancy is fatal. When unsure, choose boring.

If a sentence is high-stakes and you cannot make it sound native, flag it as low-confidence rather than shipping a confident-sounding calque.

## Section 8 — Typography and Formatting

- **Quotation marks:** `„…"` (low-open, high-close). Never `"…"`, never `«…»`. Titles of companies/media inside them when transliterated.
- **Foreign names:** in pre-2020 register, transliterate into Cyrillic inside `„…"` (`„Џапан ерлајнс"`, `„Фиат"`). In contemporary tech/finance writing, well-known brand, ticker and product names are increasingly kept in **bare Latin** (Microsoft, S&P 500, Nasdaq, SpaceX) — acceptable in modern copy; pick one convention and hold it within a text.
- **Numbers:** decimal **comma** (`10,9`), **dot** as thousands separator (`15.868`). Small round numbers spelled out (`два отсто`).
- **Percent:** word after the numeral; `отсто`, `проценти`, `посто` and `%` all occur and may rotate within one text. Percentage points = `процентни поени` / `п.п.`.
- **Currency:** `€` or `евра`; local `денари` / `МКД` / `ден.`. Large sums often restated in the other currency with `или` / `односно` (`93,5 милиони денари, односно 1,5 милиони евра`).
- **Dash:** the spaced hyphen `-` is the workhorse — headline splits, appended specifics, and quote attribution (`„…" - велат од Министерството`). In running content prefer a plain hyphen; avoid the em-dash.
- **Dates:** `ДД.ММ.ГГГГ` (`05.02.2026`) or `Февруари 2026` for month-level.
- **Time:** 24-hour, `14:30`.

## Section 9 — Marketing & Sales Register (promotional copy)

Sections 1–8 are the base: plain business Macedonian. Marketing copy (websites, ads, product pages, „За нас", CTAs) keeps all of that and **adds** the rules below. It was derived from originally-written Macedonian marketing copy of local companies — insurers, retailers, FMCG brands, fuel/energy, and local agencies — deliberately excluding the group-owned telecoms, whose Macedonian is translated from English templates and reads foreign. That exclusion is the point: **most of the "marketing Macedonian" on the web is translated and is exactly what not to imitate.**

### 9.1 `ти` or `Вие`? Register is chosen by sector and by slot

This is the first question in any marketing copy, and Macedonian answers it in a specific pattern:

- **Body copy — `Вие` or impersonal by default.** Banks, insurance, utilities, and established retail address the reader as **Вие** (`Осигурете се…`, `Изберете го вашиот пакет`). Retail chains often use no direct address at all — an impersonal company **`ние`** voice (`Работиме за вас`, `Нудиме…`).
- **`ти` for youth / tech / education brands** throughout their copy (ed-tech, startups, creative brands): `твојата иднина`, `Пријави се`, `Однеси ме до…`.
- **The slogan and the buttons flip to `ти` even on a `Вие` site.** This is the marketing-specific split English does not have: an insurance company writes its body in Вие but its ad line and its clickable buttons in the bare singular imperative — `Купи онлајн`, `Плати`, `Пријави штета`, `Дознај повеќе` — sitting right next to Вие prose. That is correct, not inconsistent.

**The rule:** default the body to **Вие** (or impersonal `ние`) for anything except a youth/tech brand; let the **slogan and the CTA buttons** drop to bare `ти`-imperative. **Never mix ти and Вие inside the same body sentence or paragraph.**

### 9.2 Frame the value as the customer's action, not "we offer"

English marketing says "We offer / We provide / We deliver." Native Macedonian turns the benefit into something the customer **does**, with a reflexive imperative:
- ✅ `Осигурете се и патувајте безгрижно.` (Insure yourself and travel carefree)
- ✅ `Заштитете се од финансиска загуба.` · `Обезбедете си врвна здравствена услуга.`
- ❌ `Ние нудиме осигурување кое ве штити…` (company-first, translated feel)

Two more native value-prop devices:
- **Reciprocity clause `вие X, а ние Y`:** `Ваше е да го сакате вашиот миленик, наше да се погрижиме за него.` (Yours is to love your pet, ours to care for it.)
- **`Затоа` reason-first hinge:** establish the need first, *then* name the product — `…затоа Витаминка го создаде…`. English introduces the product first and explains after.

### 9.3 Calls to action — two native shapes

1. **Bare imperative in the brand's register** — the punchy one, usually `ти`: `Дознај повеќе`, `Стани член`, `Погледни пакети`, `Аплицирај`, `Пријави се`, `Одбери`, `Купи онлајн`. With Вие brands the same button pluralizes: `Дознајте повеќе`, `Станете корисник`, `Аплицирајте веднаш`.
2. **Register-safe noun-CTA (no verb)** — banks and insurers prefer this: `Барање за понуда`, `Бесплатна консултација`, `Онлајн пријава`.
3. **First-person-voice buttons** are idiomatic in Macedonian and have no clean English equivalent — the button speaks *as the customer*: `Доаѓам во…`, `Вложувам во иднина`, `Однеси ме до…`.

A ready CTA verb bank: `дознај / дознајте повеќе` (learn more), `пријави се` (sign up), `аплицирај` (apply), `побарај понуда` (request a quote), `стани член / корисник` (become a member), `одбери го твојот пакет` (choose your plan), `склучи онлајн` (conclude online), `contactирај нѐ` → `контактирај нѐ денес` (contact us today), `резервирај` (book), `искористи ја можноста` (take the chance).

### 9.4 Slogans and headlines are short, verbless, and often prepositional

Native Macedonian slogans almost never use the English verb-led or "the X you deserve" noun-phrase shape. The recurring native shapes:
- **Verbless equation / declaration:** `Домот е еден.` · `Сѐ на едно место.` · `Купувачот е наш приоритет.`
- **Prepositional `Со [бренд] до [цел]`:** `Со Винер Осигурување до сигурен дом.`
- **`[Бренд], [зачестеност] со вас`:** `Тинекс, секој ден со вас!` · the intimate variant `Тинекс, ти и јас.`
- **Emotive absolute:** `Ништо не е поважно од вашето здравје.`
- **Sound-driven caps triad:** `БРЗО, ЛЕСНО, ЕДНОСТАВНО!` · `Штедиме заедно, растеме безгрижно!`
- **Clitic-fronted:** `Успешната приказна ЈА пишуваме со Вас.`

### 9.5 Trust and brand story — the native "За нас" voice

English "About us" sells competence and KPIs. Macedonian brand-story copy sells **belonging, tradition, and trust**:
- **Romanticise the founding year:** `во далечната 1999 година`, `ги отвори вратите`, `нашата приказна започна во 1994`.
- **The `успешна приказна` device** and generational belonging: `составен дел од животите на неколку генерации`.
- **First / oldest / most-recognised claims with `нај-`**, backed by a year or a hard number — `првата банка… во 1944`, `најдолга традиција`, `над 500 изработени веб-страни` — not abstract adjectives.
- **A named-CEO or founder quote**, and **local pride as a moral folk-truth:** `ако самите не го цениме нашето, нема никој да ни го цени`.
- Gratitude is framed as **earned trust**, not a transaction: `Се радуваме што ја оправдавме Вашата доверба` — never a literal "thank you for your business".

### 9.6 The marketing calque blacklist (the "решенија" plague)

Marketing is where translated-English cliché is thickest. The worst offender is **`решенија`** ("solutions") used as a blanket word for services — a dead giveaway of agency-speak translated from English. The full marketing blacklist is folded into Section 1; the highest-frequency ones to watch:

| ❌ Marketing calque | ✅ Natural Macedonian | English source |
|---|---|---|
| решенија по мерка / прилагодени решенија | услуги скроени според вас / работа прилагодена на потребите | tailored solutions |
| маркетинг / бизнис решенија | маркетинг услуги / конкретна помош | marketing / business solutions |
| го подигнете вашиот бренд | го зајакнете / издигнете брендот | elevate your brand |
| отклучите го целосниот потенцијал | го искористите целиот потенцијал | unlock the full potential |
| во денешниот дигитален пејзаж | во денешниот дигитален свет | in today's digital landscape |
| претвораме посетители во клиенти | привлекуваме купувачи / посетителите да купат | turn visitors into customers |
| длабок увид во потребите | добро ги познаваме потребите | deep insight into needs |
| шопинг искуство | искуство при купување / пазарување | shopping experience |
| таргет група | целна група | target group |
| контакт детали | податоци за контакт | contact details |
| 100% задоволни клиенти | целосно задоволни клиенти | 100% satisfied clients |
| на високо ниво | врвно / многу квалитетно | at a high level |
| се надевам дека овој меил ве наоѓа добро | (drop it) Пишувам во врска со… | hope this email finds you well |
| само сакав да проверам / да се навратам | Дали имавте можност да го разгледате… | just checking in / circling back |
| не двоумете се да ме контактирате | Ви стојам на располагање | don't hesitate to contact me |

## Section 10 — Formal Document & Proposal Register (reports, strategies, proposals)

This is the register of annual reports, strategies, official proposals, and formal business documents — the most elevated of the three. It keeps the base rules and adds a heavier, more ritualised layer. Derived from Macedonian bank annual reports, central-bank analyses, and ministry strategy documents. Use it for a formal proposal or report; do **not** use it for an email or a web page (those are lighter — see Sections 9 and 11).

### 10.1 Formal openings and closings are frozen rituals — do not calque the English ones

- **Salutation:** a bare plural vocative on its own line, ending in a **comma** (not a colon), audiences listed most-formal-first: `Почитувани акционери, деловни партнери и клиенти,`. A lone `Почитувани,` resets into a new part mid-document.
- **Never calque "Dear X":** `Драги акционери` is wrong. And there is no "I am pleased to present" opener — the CEO letter **opens by setting the year's macro scene** before any first person: `Во текот на 2024 година, економијата се соочи со значителни предизвици…`.
- **Sign-off:** `Со почит,` then the **title before the name** — `Со почит, Главен извршен директор, [Име]`. The reverse (name then title) is the English stack and reads translated.
- **Closing move:** not a valediction but a **thanks-cascade** to stakeholders and/or a rally on shared values: `…им се заблагодарувам за укажаната доверба`.

### 10.2 Objectives and measures are nominalized — verbs become verbal nouns

The backbone of formal Macedonian is the **-ње / -ција verbal noun**. English action verbs collapse into nouns:
- ✅ Objective headings are bare verbal nouns: `Цел 1. Обезбедување на…`, `Цел 2. Развој на…`, `Одржување на стабилноста`.
- ✅ The fixed goal frame: `Целта на оваа мерка е да се обезбеди…` or `Целта на овој столб е унапредување на…`.
- ✅ Purpose is `со цел / во насока на + verbal noun`, **not** a `да`-clause: `со цел зголемување на конкурентноста` (not `за да се зголеми конкурентноста`).
- Core lexicon to reach for: `спроведување, реализација, унапредување, остварување, имплементација, обезбедување, зголемување, одржување`.

English "in order to increase competitiveness" → Macedonian `со цел зголемување на конкурентноста`. This nominal density *is* the formal register; plain verbs read as too casual for a strategy document.

### 10.3 Commitment is stated indirectly — continuation, not a fresh promise

The explicit "we commit" (`се обврзуваме`) is almost absent. Institutions signal commitment three native ways:
- **Continuation:** `ќе продолжи со + verbal noun`, and the adverbs `и натаму / и понатаму` (carry most of English "will continue to"): `Народната банка и натаму ќе спроведува…`.
- **Standing disposition:** `останува посветена на…`, `останува подготвена да преземе…`.
- **Agentless analytic future:** where English says "we will create X", formal Macedonian says `ќе се создаде X` — the actor suppressed by reflexive `се`.
- The institution is named in the **third person even when it authored the document** (`Владата ќе ги преиспита…`, `Банката ќе продолжи…`) — this projects official-record authority, not the English first-person "we will."

### 10.4 Forecasts and assessments are double-hedged and agentless

A bare point forecast reads as non-institutional. The native formal forecast stacks:
1. a **circumstance-first backdrop**, front-loaded: `Во услови на зголемена неизвесност, …` (not trailed at the end as in English);
2. an **impersonal reflexive projection verb**: `се очекува / се оценува / се предвидува дека…` — never "we expect";
3. often a **trailing risk qualifier**: `…придружено со ризици`, and a concessive pivot `И покрај…, сепак…`.

`Сепак,` sentence-initial is the single most common cohesion move in this register (a genuine concession, unlike its rarity in news).

### 10.5 Formal connectors — a dense, repetitive, fixed inventory

Formal Macedonian collapses English's variety of linking phrases onto a small set of high-frequency multiword nominal connectors, and **repeats them without apology** (repetition is correct here, not stylistic weakness). By real frequency in the corpus:

| Connector | ~count | Job |
|---|---|---|
| во однос на | 900+ | regarding / compared with |
| согласно / во согласност со | 1100+ | pursuant to / in accordance with (near-mandatory when citing a law, strategy, or rule) |
| во рамките на / во рамки на | 600+ | within (the framework of) |
| со цел | 440 | in order to (+ verbal noun) |
| во услови на | 360 | amid / under conditions of (the default backdrop adverbial) |
| во насока на | 206 | toward / in the direction of |
| врз основа на | 177 | on the basis of |
| во текот на | 176 | during / in the course of |
| заради | 147 | for the purpose of / owing to |
| имајќи (го/ги) предвид | ~30 | bearing in mind (participial cohesion) |

Sentence-initial single-word glue where English starts with "Therefore/Also": `Оттука,` `Воедно,` `Притоа,` `Во таа насока,`. Note that `притоа` — flagged as bookish and near-absent in the *news* register (Section 4) — is legitimately at home here in formal documents. **Register decides:** the same word that is a foreigner-tell in a news sentence is correct in a strategy paragraph.

### 10.6 Voice splits by section

One formal document runs two voices, and switching between them is correct:
- **The signed foreword** (CEO/governor/minister address) uses **first person**: `се стремиме`, `уверен сум дека`, `остваривме`.
- **The body** (strategy, analysis, measures) switches to **impersonal reflexive**: `се предвидува`, `ќе се обезбеди`, `се оценува`.

Do not carry the first-person foreword voice into the analytical body, or the body's agentless voice into the personal address.

## Section 11 — Email & Correspondence Register

Business email is the lightest register: formal but personal, `Вие` throughout, short and direct. The biggest failure mode is opening with a translated English pleasantry. This section is codified from Macedonian business-correspondence conventions and cross-checked against the salutation/sign-off patterns validated in Section 10; treat the phrase bank as reliable but run a native ear over anything high-stakes.

### 11.1 Open with the reason, never with an English pleasantry

- ❌ `Се надевам дека овој меил ве наоѓа добро.` — there is **no native equivalent** of "I hope this email finds you well." It is a pure calque and an instant tell.
- ✅ Open directly with why you are writing: `Пишувам во врска со…` (I'm writing regarding…), `Ве контактирам поради…` (I'm contacting you about…).

### 11.2 Salutations — mind the vocative

A bare plural vocative on its own line, ending in a **comma** (never a colon):

- `Почитувани,` — the safe default to an organisation, team, or unknown recipient.
- `Почитувани колеги,` / `Почитувани клиенти,` — to a defined group.
- Named individual — **the title takes the vocative case**: `Почитуван господин**е** Петровски,` (man), `Почитувана госпоѓ**о** Трајковска,` (woman), `Почитуван професор**е**,` (academic). The surname stays unchanged.
- ❌ `Драг господине…` — `Драг` ("Dear") is too intimate for business; use `Почитуван`.
- In a live thread, drop the salutation after the first exchange or use a light `Здраво,` once — don't re-stack `Почитувани` on every reply. Stay `Вие`.

### 11.3 Sign-offs (formal → warm)

- `Со почит,` — the standard formal close. Then **title before name** (`Со почит,` / `Раководител на продажба,` / `[Име Презиме]`). Name-then-title is the English stack.
- `Со почитување,` — a heavier formal variant.
- `Срдечен поздрав,` — warm-but-professional, for an established contact.
- `Поздрав,` — neutral-casual, internal only.
- ❌ Avoid `Искрено Ваш` ("Sincerely yours") and `Со најдобри желби` ("Best wishes") as defaults — literal renderings.

### 11.4 Functional phrase bank

| MK | EN | When |
|---|---|---|
| Ве молам да ми доставите… | Please send me… | direct polite request |
| Ве замолувам за… | I kindly ask you for… | softer/formal request |
| Би сакал / Би сакала да… | I would like to… | stating intent politely |
| Дали би можеле да…? | Could you…? | softest request |

### 11.5 Register notes

- **`Вие` throughout** — never drop to `ти` in a professional thread, even after friendly exchanges, unless the other side clearly does first.
- **Courtesy capitalization:** capitalize `Вие, Вас, Ваш, Ви, Ве` when addressing the recipient respectfully — the safe formal default.
- **Impersonal reflexive `се`** adds institutional distance and softens who is demanding what: `документот да се достави до петок`.
- **Standard Cyrillic, no chat-speak** — `поздрав`, not `позз`; no emoticons, no Latin script, no slang.

## Appendix — Provenance and Confidence

- **Corpus (Sections 1–8, news register):** ~860 business/economy articles harvested from eight Macedonian outlets (news agencies, public and commercial broadcasters, and a dedicated finance portal). 435 were read in full: ~40 in a deep literal-back-translation pass and 397 in a broad validation sweep, totalling roughly 143,000 words of analysed native prose. The bulk was published before 2023 (pre-AI, guaranteed human), with a contemporary layer for current vocabulary.
- **Corpus (Section 9, marketing register):** ~40 pages of originally-written Macedonian marketing copy from local insurers, retailers, FMCG brands, fuel/energy companies, and local agencies, triaged to exclude translated (group-owned telecom) copy. Smaller and page-based rather than word-counted; treat Section 9 as well-grounded but less exhaustively validated than Sections 1–8.
- **Corpus (Section 10, formal-document register):** ~24 Macedonian formal documents — bank annual-report CEO letters, National Bank analyses and financial-stability reports, and government/ministry strategy and program documents — read for their narrative prose (tables excluded). Institutional Macedonian is originally-written, so translation risk is low; the connector frequencies cited are real counts from these documents.
- **Corpus (Section 11, email register):** codified from Macedonian business-correspondence and email-etiquette conventions, cross-checked against the Section 10 salutation/sign-off findings. The salutation, sign-off, vocative, and capitalization rules are well-attested; the functional phrase bank is reliable but was not drawn from a single authoritative corpus — a native ear is the final gate for high-stakes wording.
- **Method:** rules were derived from the deep pass, then validated against the full broad sweep. The complete validation killed no rule and confirmed the load-bearing ones (clitic doubling, the connector finding, trailing attribution, renarrated past, number grammar) across 100+ texts each — these are high-confidence. A few (scheduled-present tense, `сè додека` negation) rest on fewer examples and are marked in-text as tendencies — reliable but not exceptionless. Two rules are register-dependent and the guide flags both readings: foreign brand names may stay in Latin in contemporary copy (Section 8), and foreign institutions may take the subject slot English-style (Section 3.2).
- **What this file deliberately excludes:** personal style preferences of any individual writer. It teaches the *language*, not a house voice. Layer your own tone on top.
- **Contributing:** the highest-value contribution is a **caught calque** — a phrase an AI produced that no native would say — in the form `bad phrase → natural phrase → English source`, added to the Section 1 blacklist. Corrections are paid for once and should never be paid for twice.
