# Contributing

Thank you for helping make AI-written business Macedonian sound native.

## The most valuable contribution: a caught calque

If you catch an AI writing a phrase that no Macedonian would actually say, that is gold. Report it in this exact form:

| ❌ Calque (what the AI wrote) | ✅ Natural Macedonian | English source |
|---|---|---|
| the bad phrase | the phrase a native would use | the English expression it came from |

The fastest way: use the **["Report a calque" issue form](../../issues/new?template=calque-report.yml)** — it asks exactly these three things. Or open a pull request adding the row to the blacklist in **Section 1** of `BUSINESS-MACEDONIAN.md`. One phrase per report. The English source matters — it explains *why* the AI produced the mistake.

## Other useful contributions

- **Phrase bank entries** (Section 5): a natural business collocation with its English meaning. Please only add phrases you have actually seen in native business writing or heard from native speakers — never a literal translation from English.
- **Connector frequency corrections** (Section 4): if you have evidence a connector is more or less common than stated, say so with examples.
- **New patterns** (Sections 2, 3, 6): a native construction the guide misses, with a short example (max ~15 words) and its literal English gloss so non-speakers can follow.

## Ground rules

- **Short snippets only.** Examples are illustrations of a pattern, not reproductions of any source text. Keep examples under ~15 words. Do not paste whole articles, paragraphs, or copyrighted passages.
- **Native, not literal.** The whole point is to escape word-for-word translation. Every suggestion should be something a native actually says.
- **Business register.** This guide covers economy, entrepreneurship, management, and finance. Patterns from other domains may not generalize.
- **Explain the why.** A correction with its English source and a one-line reason is far more useful than a bare fix.

## How suggestions are accepted

Contributions arrive as issues or pull requests and are reviewed before merging. Nothing changes the published guide without maintainer approval.
